Constraints


While collecting primary and secondary data and also creating a Multimodal digital solution how the inclusion of the AFLW has led to greater participation rates amongst girls playing Australian Rules football, there may be a number of limiting factors attributable to the hypothesis that I am testing. In terms of:


Economic constraints for my hypothesis, I may lack funds for hosting a website and domain name when presenting my findings on my hypothesis. The time applied will be a constraint as well because I have 10 weeks to complete my SAT of which I will need to dedicate a lot of time and study into completing this SAT. The will also be another problem with trying to interview as much people as possible in a short given space of time, this means that I will only be able to interview a set number of people which means I wonâ€™t be able to see much variance in data (may only be one-sided?)

Technical constraints for this solution include my availability of equipment, because my solution may only be used on a web device and may not fit the conventions of responsive web design, as well as my findings with primary and secondary data, I may not have the capacity to store all data in an excel spreadsheet on all my findings. The compatibility and security as another technical constraint because I should allow my solution users to verify their identity when filling out my surveys when I am collecting primary data. This means that my findings have a better chance of being reasonable and freedom from bias.

Social constraints include the level of expertise within people giving me secondary data, for example, I could have a situation where the primary data collectors do not know enough about the issue and give me false findings, which will not represent a reasonable MMOS. Legal Constraints include the ownership of the data collected, because the data needs to be owned by the organisation that is commissioning the data. This means that I will need to reference the data using the Harvard referencing system. In addition, the data needs to have privacy of data requirements. All data used in the solution must be de-identified to protect respondents, as well as abide by APPs.

Usability constraints include the usefulness of the data collected and the MMOS. The Solution should use images and words to expand vocabulary of users that may struggle with basic Australian English standards. The solution needs to have an ease of use, meaning that once the data is collected and presented as information, it should be simple for users to understand the data collected as it is presented in clear graphs and figures with text.
